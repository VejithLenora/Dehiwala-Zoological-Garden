ANIMALS

Catfish

Catfish are a type of fish in the taxonomic order Siluriformes. They are easily identifiable by their cat-like whiskers. Scientists have identified over 30 families of Catfish.

Cheetah

Cheetahs are well known as the fastest animal on land. Cheetahs are the perfect hunters not just because of their amazing speed but also because of their exceptional eyesight.

Elephant

The largest land animal in earth. They are easily identifiable by their long trunks and large ears and size.

King Cobra

The King Cobra belonging to the family Elapidae is not really a cobra. It is well known for its deadly venom and size. They are mostly found in India and SouthEast-India

Komodo Dragon

Often referred to as land Crocodile the Komodo Dragon is the largest living lizard. They are easily recognizable by their massive size, flat heads and thick tails.

Leopards

Leopard, (Panthera pardus) also referred to commonly as panther is a large cat closely related to Lions, Tigers and Jaguars.

Lion

The second largest member of the cat family the Lion, (Panthera leo) is very muscularly built. They are one of the most recognizable animals in the world.

Mugger Crocodile

Commonly found in India and surrounding countries the Marsh crocodile is one of the three crocodiles found in India. These are very strong creatures capable of easily overpowering a Lion.

Plants

Name Appearance Uses
Hair Water Lily Leaves float on the water's surface and are nearly circular in shape. They are notched to the center and the leaf lobes are pointed. The leaves are on the tops of long stalks that extend from long rhizomes in the mud. Fragrant water lily flowers are showy, white and aromatic. Hair water Lilies are often given as offerings during prayers and their oils are also extracted due to its fragrance.
Tabebuia Rosea Leaves-palmately compound, leaflets-elliptic, pointed ends.Trunk- bark-rough, furrowed, grey. Flowers:-pink, showy. Inflorescence -terminal and axillary corymbs. Fruits- dark brown, cigar-like capsules. The Tabebuia Rosea is valued and used for its wood. The wood is perfect for wood-heavy construction and is also used as a shade tree.
Terminalia Arjuna Tree with a broad base and smooth, grey bark. Leaves are placed in near opposites, and are about 5-13 cm long and 2- 5 cm broad, oval in shape with a rounded base and blunt tip, short-petiolated with a pair of glands about 1 mm immediately below the base of lamina. Leaves turn bright red when old. Flowers are small, sessile, cream to greenish-white, on rachis about 9-13 cm of branched inflorescences borne terminally or at nodes. The fruit is elliptic 3-5 cm long, 2-3 cm wide, five-winged, woody structure. The tree can be used to purify water and can also be used for its wood.
Tamarindus indica The greyish-brown trunk is finely grooved vertically and rough. The leaves are finely compound, with 8-20 pairs of leaflets that are 12-30 mm long and 3-10 mm broad, arranged in opposites, each oblong in shape with a rounded base and apex. Flowers are about 2 cm, pale yellow, borne in clusters up to 8. The fruit is a grey-brown fleshy pod up to 20 cm long and 1-1.5 cm thick, consisting of dark brown pulp at maturity and 2-4 round, compressed, shiny brown seeds. Native tree. Fruit pulp used as a condiment. A valuable timber, often grown in home gardens.
